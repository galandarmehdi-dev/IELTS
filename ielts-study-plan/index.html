Planning

The best IELTS plan mixes mocks, drills, and review.

Taking test after test without review can feel productive, but it often repeats the same mistakes. A good weekly plan gives each section a clear job.

A simple weekly routine

Day 1: Full mock

Take a complete mock or one full skill section. Record the score and the question types that caused problems.

Day 2-3: Targeted drills

Practise the weak question types from the mock: headings, maps, sentence completion, or essay planning.

Day 4: Writing review

Rewrite one Task 1 or Task 2 paragraph and focus on one criterion at a time.

How much practice is enough?

Students with a near target band may need fewer full mocks and more precise review. Students far from the target need language improvement alongside exam technique. If your score is stuck, look at the reason: vocabulary, grammar, timing, question interpretation, or writing development.

A balanced plan includes at least one review session for every test session. The review is where the score begins to change.

Use mocks intelligently

Do a full mock when you need stamina and realistic pressure. Do section practice when one skill is weak. Do short drills when a specific question type keeps going wrong.
