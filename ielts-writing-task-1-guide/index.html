Task 1

A good report is selective, not exhaustive.

Task 1 is not a list of every number. It is a short report that identifies the main features, groups information logically, and supports comparisons with accurate data.

Recommended structure

Introduction

Paraphrase the task. Keep it short and factual. Do not add reasons, opinions, or predictions.

Overview

State the biggest trends, highest and lowest points, or main contrast. This is essential for Task Achievement.

Details

Use two body paragraphs to group related data. Compare instead of listing values one by one.

What to include in the overview

For line charts, look for overall increases, decreases, fluctuations, and final rankings. For bar charts, compare the largest and smallest categories. For maps, describe major changes in land use, access, buildings, and open space. For processes, summarise the number of stages and the start/end point.

Avoid exact numbers in the overview unless the chart is very simple. Save detailed figures for the body paragraphs.

Common Task 1 mistakes

Many students lose marks by writing too much background, missing the overview, copying the prompt too closely, using inaccurate data, or making unsupported claims. Another common problem is grammar control: a report with good ideas can still lose marks if tense, articles, and sentence boundaries are unstable.
