Band scores

Your score is useful only if it changes your next practice decision.

A mock result is not just a number. It should show which section needs attention, which question types are unstable, and whether your timing is exam-ready.

Listening and Reading

Listening and Reading are scored from 40 questions. The raw score is converted to a band. A higher raw score means a higher band, but the exact conversion can vary by test difficulty. This is why students should track patterns across several mocks rather than judging progress from one attempt.

Writing

Writing is judged by criteria such as task response or achievement, coherence and cohesion, lexical resource, and grammatical range and accuracy. For Task 1, the overview and data selection are especially important. For Task 2, the position and development of ideas matter heavily.

How to use your result

Find the weakest section

Compare Listening, Reading, and Writing rather than only looking at the overall score.

Look for repeated errors

A repeated question-type mistake is more important than a one-off careless error.

Retest after targeted work

Use another mock only after reviewing and practising the problem area.
