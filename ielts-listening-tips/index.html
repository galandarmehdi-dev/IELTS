IELTS Listening

Prediction is the skill before listening.

Most listening errors happen before the answer is spoken: the student has not predicted the type of answer, the likely topic, or the direction of the conversation.

Before the audio starts

Use the preparation time to read the questions and mark what kind of answer is expected. Is it a name, price, date, place, opinion, letter, or direction? If a gap follows an adjective, a noun is likely. If the question asks for a reason, listen for cause language such as because, due to, or as a result.

For maps and diagrams, locate the entrance, compass direction, left/right turns, and fixed landmarks before listening. For multiple choice, read the options quickly and expect paraphrases rather than exact words.

Common Listening traps

Corrections

Speakers often give one answer and then correct it. The second version is usually the answer.

Plural forms

A missing final -s can change a correct answer into a wrong one. Check countable nouns carefully.

Distractors

Wrong options are often mentioned in the audio. Listen for the speaker's final choice, not just any matching word.

How to improve after a mock

Review the transcript if available, but do it in stages. First replay the section without the transcript and try to hear the missed answer. Then use the transcript to locate why you missed it: speed, pronunciation, paraphrase, spelling, or distractor.

Your practice should include full listening tests for stamina and short section practice for weak question types. Do not only repeat full tests; targeted review is where most improvement happens.
