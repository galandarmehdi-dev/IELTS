Task 2

Strong essays are clear before they are complex.

IELTS Task 2 rewards a clear answer, developed reasoning, paragraph control, and accurate language. Complex vocabulary cannot rescue an essay with an unclear position.

Plan before writing

Spend a few minutes identifying the question type and your position. Decide your two main ideas before you write the introduction. A simple plan prevents repetition and keeps the essay focused.

For opinion essays, your position should be visible from the introduction. For problem-solution essays, each problem should connect logically to a solution. For discussion essays, represent both sides fairly before giving your own view.

Body paragraph formula

Topic sentence

Begin with the main idea of the paragraph, not a memorised phrase.

Explanation

Explain why the idea matters and how it answers the question.

Example

Use a realistic example that supports the point. It does not need to be a statistic.

What raises the band score

Higher-scoring essays usually have a consistent position, enough development, clear paragraphing, and varied but controlled grammar. Vocabulary should be precise rather than decorative. It is better to use a normal word accurately than a rare word incorrectly.

After every practice essay, review one issue at a time: task response, paragraph logic, linking, sentence grammar, or vocabulary accuracy. Trying to fix everything in one rewrite often leads to confusion.
