IELTS Reading

Read for evidence, not for every word.

The IELTS Reading test rewards controlled searching, careful matching, and calm timing. A good reader in IELTS is not someone who translates every sentence; it is someone who can find proof quickly and avoid traps.

Build a passage routine

Start each passage by reading the title, subtitle, first paragraph, and final paragraph. Then scan the question set before reading deeply. This gives you a map of what information you are likely to need.

For most students, the biggest timing problem is over-reading at the beginning. Spend enough time to understand the passage structure, but do not try to memorize every detail. Question types such as sentence completion, matching names, and true/false/not given all send you back to specific parts of the text.

Question-type advice

Matching headings

Read the whole paragraph for its main function. Do not choose a heading only because it contains a repeated word.

True / False / Not Given

Ask whether the text directly confirms, directly contradicts, or simply does not say the idea. Do not use outside knowledge.

Sentence completion

Predict the grammar of the missing word first: noun, adjective, verb, number, or name. Then check spelling and word limit.

How to review Reading mistakes

After a mock test, group wrong answers by reason: wrong location, misunderstood question, vocabulary problem, grammar of the gap, or rushed guess. This makes review useful. If you only read the answer key, the same mistake will usually return in the next test.

A strong review routine is: find the sentence that proves the answer, underline the phrase that misled you, write the paraphrase in your own words, and repeat the same question type the next day.
